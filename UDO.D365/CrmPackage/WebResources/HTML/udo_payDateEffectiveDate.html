Pay Date / Effective Date

Veteran's claim received < 1 year from RAD Veteran's claim received > 1 year from RAD
    Because we received your claim within one year of your release from active duty (RAD), the effective date of your claim is the day following your RAD.Your RAD is ____, so your effective date is the first of the month following your discharge date, which is ____. Your payment entitlement date is the first of the following month. You will actually receive this money the first of the next month (because VA pays one month behind).
    Example:
    RAD: April 30
    Effective date: May 1
    Payment entitlement date: June 1
    Actual pay date: July 1
    Because we received your claim more than one year after your date of discharge, your date of claim (DOC) is your effective date.Your payment entitlement date is the first of the following month. You will actually receive this money the first of the next month (because VA pays one month behind).
    Example:
    RAD: April 30, 2007
    DOC: July 5, 2008 (more than 1 yr. after RAD
    Effective date: July 5, 2008
    Payment entitlement date: August 1, 2008
    Actual pay date: September 1, 2008

Pay Date on Weekend/Holiday

    VA pays benefits on the first of each month for the previous month. But if the first falls on a weekend or holiday, payment is the last business day of the previous month.
    Example: In 2008, March 1 is a Saturday, so benefits would be paid on Feb. 29 (Friday).
