    1. What is eBenefits?
    The eBenefits portal was developed by the Department of Defense (DoD) and the Department of Veterans Affairs to be a web based one-stop shop for online benefits-related tools and information for Wounded Warriors, Veterans, Service Members, their families, and their caretakers.
    After you create an account, you can use my eBenefits to set up a profile, add and manage favorite links, access military personnel records such as your DD 214, and view healthcare information, and more.
    Explore eBenefits to learn more about the available self-service features. Additional capabilities are always in development, so check back often. To get the most of your eBenefits experience, you will need to obtain a DoD Self Service (DS) Logon.
    Back to Top
    2. What is a DS Logon?
    DS Logon is a secure, self-service logon ID that allows Beneficiaries affiliated with the DoD or the VA access to several websites using a single username and password. If you are already registered in DEERS, you are eligible for a DS Logon. Once you have a DS Logon, it's valid for the rest of your life.
    A DS Logon supports the Personnel Identity Protection Directive and National Institute of Standards and Technology e-Authentication guidance. It provides a high level of authentication assurance in situations where Common Access Card authentication is not available.
    Back to Top
    3. What is a Basic (eBenefits) DS Logon Account?
    A Basic DS Logon Basic account is an entry-level user account established online that provides limited access to your personal information. This level of account is only provided to individuals who have registered online on the eBenefits website without being in-person proofed. Many applications within eBenefits will not allow access with a Basic account.
    Back to Top
    4. What is a Premium (eBenefits) DS Logon Account?
    A DS Logon Premium account is given to a user who has registered online through an identity proofing process or an in-person proofing process with a VA or DoD agency official. A Premium account is a higher level of validation and assurance as it enables eBenefits users access to personally identifiable information and unlimited access to all eBenefits features.
    Back to Top
    5. How do I get a DS Logon (eBenefits) Premium account if I am a Service member?
    No. If you are an active duty service member, having a Common Access Card verifies that you have been in-person proofed.

    6. I am an active Service member with a Common Access Card. Do I need to be In- Person Proofed?
    If you have a Common Access Card you may request a DS Logon for yourself and eligible family members via the DS Access Center at https://myaccess.dmdc.osd.mil/dsaccess/.
    You may also visit the eBenefits Registration webpage for other ways to obtain a DS Logon account.
    Back to Top
    7. How do I get a DS Logon (eBenefits) Premium account if I am a military retiree?
    If you have a DFAS myPay Login identification, you may request a DS Logon for yourself and eligible family members via the DS Access Center at https://myaccess.dmdc.osd.mil/dsaccess/.
    You may also visit the eBenefits Registration webpage for other ways to obtain a DS Logon account.
    Back to Top
    8. How do I get a DS Logon (eBenefits) Premium account if I am a Veteran?
    You can obtain a DS Logon Basic account by registering online via eBenefits . To obtain a Premium account, you can register in-person, on the telephone, or online depending on your status. See the links below to determine your best route to obtaining a Premium account.
    Back to Top
    9. What is in-person proofing?
    In-person proofing is a method of verifying the identity of a Veteran, Service Member, or dependent. It requires that you appear in person at a VA regional office or DoD TRICARE facility located on a military installation and verify your identity.
    Back to Top
    10. Why is in-person proofing necessary for a DS Logon Premium account?
    In-person proofing ensures that you are who you say you are. It also prevents an imposter from using your name to create a fraudulent DS Logon account.
    Back to Top
    11. Where can I be in-person proofed for a DS Logon (eBenefits) Premium account and what information will I need to bring?
    In person proofing is currently performed at designated VA Regional Offices or DoD TRICARE facilities. You will need to bring the following identification with you to be in-person proofed.
  • At least one form of photo identification issued by a federal or state government. This includes a state-issued driver's license, identification card, or U.S. passport (expired or unexpired).
  • A secondary form of identification. This includes a birth certificate, social security card, or picture ID from a non-governmental entity (for example, a university identification).
  • If the address on your identification items doesn�t match the address in DEERS, you need to bring proof of residency. This can be can be a utility bill, phone bill, financial statement, mortgage voucher, or rent voucher.
    Back to Top
    Can I obtain a DS Logon (eBenefits) Premium account over the telephone?
    Yes, you can obtain an eBenefits Premium account using the telephone proofing process. You must be receiving VA monetary benefit payments via electronic funds transfer. Your monetary payments can be compensation, pension, education, or vocational rehabilitation. To telephone proof, you must provide the following information:
  • Full name (Last name used while in service may be different than what is provided);
  • Social Security Number;
  • Checking or Savings Account Number and Routing Number;
  • Dollar amount of your most recent electronic fund transfer.
    Back to Top
    How do I request a DS Logon (eBenefits) Premium account using my MyHealtheVet account?
    To obtain your new or upgraded DS Logon (eBenefits) Premium account, you must use the DS Logon Account Request Portal and follow the steps below.
    1. Go to http://www.myhealth.va.gov/mhv-dslogon-portal-web/dslogonauth.portal and sign in with your In-Person Authenticated My HealtheVet credentials.
    2. Click on the DS Logon Account Request tab.
    3. Check the appropriate boxes to verify and acknowledge use of your personal information.
    4. Click Request DS Logon account.
    5. Receive your DS Logon activation code.
    6. Go to https://www.dmdc.osd.mil/appj/dsaccess/ and select Activate a DS Logon.
    7. Follow the on-screen instructions to activate your DS Logon.
    8. Record your username and password. Then return to eBenefits and click Log in.
    Note: If you are upgrading from a Basic DS Logon, once you receive your Premium DS Logon activation code, your Basic account will be immediately deactivated.
    Back to Top
    Can a Spouse or Dependent of a Veteran obtain a DS Logon?
    A spouse or dependent of a Service member or Veteran may request a DS Logon at a DoD RAPIDS facility when obtaining a military identification card or at a VA regional office after completing an in-person proofing process.
    The spouse or dependent of a DoD Sponsor may also initiate a DS Logon request via the DS Access Center via https://myaccess.dmdc.osd.mil/dsaccess/.
    A family member may choose one of the following options:
  • Spouse or family member of a service member: Have the DoD sponsor request a DS Logon on behalf of the family member.
  • Dependent age 18 or older: If the dependent has an ID card or having it replaced and you has the proper documentation, a DS Logon may be requested without having a sponsor present.
    Back to Top
    15. What if I am not in DEERs?
    Enrollment in DEERS is automatic for all Service Members and Veterans who have served since 1982. Those who served prior to 1982 are being added from VA/DoD records.
    If you find that you're not registered in DEERS or are simply not sure, call the DEERS Support Office toll-free telephone number:
  • 800-527-5602 for Alaska and Hawaii
  • 800-334-4162 for California
  • 800-538-9552 for All Other States
    Note: The best time to call the DEERS office is between 0600 and 1500 PST.

    Once you receive your DS Logon activation code, via the mail in about 5-12 business days,go to the DoD Self-Service Access Center, then click Activate a DoD Self-Service Logon.

    17. What if I do not use my DS Logon (eBenefits) account for 12 months? Will I need to get in-person proofed again?
    No, you do not need to be in-person proofed again. The DoD Self-Service Logon credential does not expire. However, if the account is not used for 150 days, the password will expire. To log on to the account again, the password must be reset by answering the challenge questions that were chosen during the initial setup of the account.
    Back to Top

    Note: If you don�t know your username, contact us (see below for contact information). If you are successful the screen will display a message to "Change Password."
    "Change Password" Message: To successfully change your password:
    1. Reenter your preset password (DOB formatted as MMDDYYYY).
    2. Enter your new password � twice.

    2. Why is ethnicity required on the eBenefits profile?
    Answer: This particular information is needed to identify a benefit based on Home Loan Guaranty�s only ethnic-based benefit for Native American Veterans. The Native American Direct Loan Program provides home loans to eligible Native American Veterans who wish to purchase, construct, or improve a home on Federal trust land.

    3. If the Veteran changes his address in eBenefits, will this change affect all benefits?
    Answer: The eBenefits portal will update the C&P Corporate record. Also, it will update the Post 9/11 GI Bill and C&P electronic payment (Direct Deposit) mailing addresses. All other VA records, such as VACOLS, medical center systems, loan guaranty, insurance, and Vocational Rehabilitation addresses cannot be updated or changed in eBenefits. The Veteran must continue to contact the regional office or medical center for all other address changes
    Back to Top
    4. Why the connection of eBenefits and DEERS?
    Answer: The DS Logon registration process (i.e., issuance of Basic/Premium accounts) is issued by DoD and requires Veterans and Service Members to be registered in DEERS. The DEERS enrollment is a requirement of DoD in order to grant access to a DS Logon.

Public Contact Representatives - 12/21/10

    1. Where can Veterans be in-person proofed for an eBenefits Premium (Level 2) account?
    Answer: Veterans can be in-person proofed at any regional office or outbased benefit office by authorized VA personnel.
    DoD recently implemented issuance of the DS Logon (Level 2) to Veterans at 85 TRICARE regional offices throughout the country. In addition, DoD agreed to accept Veterans who are non-TRICARE patients to complete the required in-person proofing for obtaining a Level 2 DS Logon account.
    Note: Veterans can only be in-person proofed at a VA medical center or clinic for My HealtheVet access, but not eBenefits.
    Back to Top
    2. Is there an alternate method of in-person proofing for Veterans who cannot visit a regional office or outbased benefit office?
    Answer: Yes, there are alternative ways in which a Veteran can obtain a Premium (Level 2) account. These methods include remote person proofing, or if the Veteran is a My HealtheVet account holder, using the Account Request Portal (ARP) to request a DS Logon (Level 2) account.
    The remote proofing process is only available to Veterans who are in receipt of an award via direct deposit. This new process is piloted at the Columbia NCC. To complete the remote proofing process, the Veteran must validate certain forms of identification, such as the checking or savings account number of the direct deposit, the name of the financial institution or routing number, and the exact payment amount, all of which is in addition to the basic ID protocol requirements. The Veteran will also be required to provide information on one primary form of identification (e.g., federal or state government ID, passport, Sponsor�s ID card, etc.)
    In order to use ARP, the Veteran must have an upgraded (in-person authenticated) My HealtheVet account. The Veteran can either request a new Premium DS Logon (Level 2) or update an existing Basic (Level 1) account to a new Premium (Level 2) account. To request or upgrade to a Premium DS Logon (Level 2), the Veteran can go to http://www.myhealth.va.gov/mhv-dslogon-portal-web/dslogonauth.portal and sign in with his In-Person Authenticated My HealtheVet credentials and click on the "DS Logon Account Request" tab, and then follow the prompts. Or, visit the eBenefits Registration page at https://www.ebenefits.va.gov/ebenefits-portal/ebenefits.portal?_nfpb=true&_nfxr=false&_pageLabel=RegistrationHelp for guidance on how to use ARP.
    In addition to the methods above, a Veteran who is a retiree and has a DFAS myPay Login ID and password can obtain Premium access using his DFAS myPay account. On the eBenefits registration page, the myPay Login ID can be used to access the DoD Self-Service Access Center. After successfully answering challenge questions, the site will generate an activation code, which will be mailed to the Veteran�s current mailing address within 6-12 days.
    Back to Top
    3. Can incompetent Veterans access their eBenefits account to change address information or generate letters?
    Answer: Incompetent Veterans cannot change their address, but they can generate letters.
    Back to Top
    4. If the Veteran changes his/her address in eBenefits, will the payment, VACOLS, and VA medical center addresses change as well?
    Answer: No. eBenefits allows the Veteran to change the compensation/pension benefit mailing address or the Post-9/11 GI Bill benefit mailing address. Since eBenefits does not link to VACOLS or the VA medical centers (VAMC), the Veteran must notify the National Call Center and the VAMC for his/her address to be updated.
    Back to Top
    5. What is the timeline for corporate information to be viewed in eBenefits?
    Answer: Veterans can view information stored in corporate records immediately within eBenefits. (The Veteran is unlikely to view information from corporate that is not available to call agents.)
    Back to Top
    6. Can eBenefits generated letters be saved to a disk or drive?
    Answer: Yes. Generated letters can be saved, but not altered, due to a digital watermark. They are saved in HTML format.
    Back to Top
    7. Does eBenefits inform Veterans about the claims process?
    Answer: Yes. eBenefits explains the three phases of the claims process in a short, easy-to-read narrative relating to the development phase, decision phase, and notification phase.
