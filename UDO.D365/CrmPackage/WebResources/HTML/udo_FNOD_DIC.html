First Notice of Death

Death of a Beneficiary Receiving Benefits in His/Her Own Right (i.e., widow, helpless child, dependent parent, Chapter 35 child) reported

    I'm sorry for your loss. I know this is a difficult time, and I'm going to help you in every way I can. In order to do that, I'll need the veteran's file number or Social Security number.
    Complete FNOD 119 and attach an encrypted e-mail and route to ROJ via PCT mailbox for award action. In the subject line, enter "Immediate Award Action Required." Discuss Status of Payments and National Cemetery Benefits for dependents as shown on Death Related Checklist.
    Note: An FNOD is performed for a deceased veteran only. Do not perform an FNOD for the death of a beneficiary as an award is needed to terminate benefits.
    Just to make you aware, the law requires that the check issued for the month in which the beneficiary died will have to be returned in addition to any subsequent checks that may be released. See PCR Index, under D for Death Checklist).
    Is there anything else I can help you with today?" [Close with empathetic closing, such as: "Please call us back if we can help you in any way." Do not close with "Have a nice day." Do not send Burial Fact Sheet].
