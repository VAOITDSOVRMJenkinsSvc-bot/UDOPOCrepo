Identification Protocol

    Once you've determined that your caller is entitled to receive privacy-protected information, you must ask the following questions:
All Callers
  • Veteran's Claim or Social Security Number
  • Veteran's Full name
  • Branch of Service
      • In addition, the following questions must be asked and verified to process a change of address/direct deposit:
    Change of Address and/or New Direct Deposit Change of Direct Deposit
  • Complete Address of Record (street number, street name, city, state, and zip code)
    		•
  • Account Number of Record
    • Current Check Amount (exact dollar amt.)
    • Note: Not necessary to ask Current Check Amount if the caller hasn't received a first recurring payment
    • Current Check Amount (exact dollar amt.)
    • Note: Not necessary to ask Current Check Amount if the caller hasn't received a first recurring payment.
    • Date of Birth
    • Date of Birth
    • Verify Caller is Calling from an Unrestricted or Unblocked Telephone Number
    • Verify Caller is Calling from an Unrestricted or Unblocked Telephone Number
      Note: You must verify the above responses in Share. Additional questions may be asked if you are not comfortable with the above responses; however, you may not substitute any of the above questions if the caller is unable to provide the correct response.
      For changes of address/direct deposit if you can't comfortably identify the caller:
    • Apologize for not being able to change the address and or direct deposit information over the telephone and advise the caller of the appropriate option below:
    • Option 1.  If the caller is a Veteran, ask if he/she has a Premium Account on eBenefits
      • If the Veteran states he/she has  a Premium Account, advise the Veteran that the change can be processed online using their eBenefits account
      • If the Veteran states he does not have a Premium Account, advise the Veteran of the benefits of having an eBenefits account and provide enrollment options
    • Option 2.  Send VA Form 20-572, Request for Change of Address/Cancellation of Direct Deposit, and/or VA Form 24-0296, Direct Deposit Enrollment Form, with any written instructions needed to complete the forms.  Tell the caller to return them to the office of jurisdiction and provide the address. 
      Hints: If the caller is unsure, provide helpful hints about information he/she should have readily available. Some examples of helpful hints include:
    • Have you received a decision or letter from us recently that may contain the information that would help you answer the questions?
    • Do you have access to a recent bank statement?
      Reminders:
    • Powers of attorney (POA, VSO, CVSO) and VA assigned payees (Fiduciary, Guardian, Custodian) are considered first-party callers and must be asked the same questions.
    • Verify POAs in BIRLS/Corp Record and using the OGC Accreditation Website on the PCR Index.
